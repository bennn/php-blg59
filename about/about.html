About

I'm Ben. Temporary research specialist at Cornell University.

Lately I've been working on object-oriented type systems with Ross Tate. We're working together with friends in industry to bring the joys of a decidable type system to a sad lonely world. I know Tony Hoare wrote that the willingness of a computer to "accept almost any sequence of instructions and make sense of them at its own level" is something that should be "cherished", but I'm a fan of syntactic discipline.

But the trouble with object-oriented type systems is that I'm not really sure what an object is. Ross says its a collection of data with methods to manipulate the data, but I don't know about that. What about self?

My likes include functional programming (♥ OCaml), verification, type theory, and adjoints.

My dislikes include nested logarithms and television.
